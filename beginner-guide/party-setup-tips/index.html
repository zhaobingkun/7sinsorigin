Party Setup Tips

A step-by-step framework for building stable teams with stronger burst uptime and cleaner role coverage.

Step 1: Decide Your Main Element

Your team efficiency is strongly tied to how reliably you can trigger elemental burst windows. Pick one core element first and keep most of your active roster aligned to that element.

Step 2: Choose Your Main DPS

After locking your element, assign one hero as your true main DPS. Raw damage matters, but controller comfort also matters because this character will carry most of your combat time.

Step 3: Add Same-Element Supports

Fill the rest of your lineup with supports that share the same element and have passives that activate during burst phases. This increases off-field value and smooths overall damage flow.

Step 4: Prioritize Gauge Utility

When deciding between similar support picks, favor heroes that help generate switch gauge or burst gauge. Faster cycling means more frequent power windows and safer pacing in longer fights.

Practical Build Checklist

  • One main element
  • One main DPS with your best upgrade path
  • Two to three supports that feed burst/crit/debuff value
  • A rotation order you can repeat reliably
