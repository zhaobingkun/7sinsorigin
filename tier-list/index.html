7DS Origin Tier List (Early Build)

Use this page as a launch-prep tier list, not a final balance verdict. The goal is to explain how we sort carries, supports, and utility picks before the live meta fully settles.

How to Read This Tier List

This page is built for launch planning. We rank characters by account value, role flexibility, setup cost, and how safely they fit into early teams. A strong pick is not just damage; it is also consistency, support value, and how easy it is to build around.

Last reviewed: April 9, 2026.

What Can Change Fast

  • Patch notes that adjust cooldowns or scaling
  • Event rewards that change how easy certain builds are to finish
  • New character reveals that replace a current support slot
  • Mode-specific findings after more players reach endgame systems

Best Use Case

Use this tier list after reading the character page and before committing resources. It works best as a direction check: who deserves priority materials, who is a flexible support, and who is more niche than they first appear.

S Tier

Reliable core picks with strong carry value, broad team fit, or universal support that stays useful across multiple early-game scenarios.

A Tier

Strong options that can look excellent with the right partner, element lane, or encounter type, but are slightly harder to slot everywhere.

B Tier

Situational picks, budget options, or characters that are fine for progression but usually need more help or a narrower setup window.

How We Rank Characters

  • Account value: Does the character help many teams or only one setup?
  • Role clarity: Is it obvious what the character is supposed to do in your rotation?
  • Team demand: Does the pick require rare partners to feel complete?
  • Patch resilience: Will the character likely stay useful if numbers move slightly?
